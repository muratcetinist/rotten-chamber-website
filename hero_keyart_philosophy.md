# Hero Key Art Brief

## Goal

Create a website hero image that sells the game in one glance:

- civic power
- hidden corruption
- deduction over action
- premium, intentional atmosphere

This image is not a splash screen for the game UI. It is a marketing image for the website.

## Core Message

The player should immediately feel:

- "This is about a council or institution."
- "Something is wrong beneath the official surface."
- "I will investigate, compare clues, and expose corruption."

The image should feel tense and intelligent, not loud or chaotic.

## What To Show

- A circular council chamber, council table, or institutional interior
- Architecture that feels governmental: stone, marble, brass, wood, seals, tall windows, official desks
- Evidence of human presence without needing a full cast close-up
- Subtle asymmetry: one chair slightly misaligned, one light left on, one missing file, one red-marked document
- A clear focal point that holds attention even when the image is seen small

## What Not To Show

- Explicit spoilers about who is corrupt
- Clown makeup, masks, exaggerated villain faces, or "twist revealed" imagery
- Generic detective cliches like string boards covering the whole frame
- Action-game energy such as explosions, combat poses, or heroic characters facing camera
- Busy collage layouts made from many unrelated elements
- Neon cyberpunk, fantasy, or comic-book styling

## Composition Rules

- Compose for website use first, not poster use first
- Keep the left or center-left area relatively calm so title, subtitle, and CTA can sit on top
- Put the main focal structure toward the center-right or deep center
- Prefer large readable shapes over many tiny details
- Use depth: foreground shadow, midground table/chamber, background architecture
- The image should read clearly at both full width and cropped mobile formats

## Mood And Lighting

- One dominant light source only: desk lamp, overhead chamber light, or cold daylight through tall windows
- Light should reveal structure, not fully explain the whole room
- Shadows should create uncertainty, not visual mud
- The room should feel expensive, official, and slightly unsafe

## Visual Language

- Institutional order that is beginning to crack
- Clean geometry disturbed by one or two suspicious details
- Circular motifs are good: table, seal, seating pattern, overhead architecture
- Use official materials: brass, stamped paper, polished wood, stone, dark fabric, glass
- If papers or records appear, they should feel archival and deliberate, not messy

## Color Direction

Base palette:

- deep navy
- charcoal
- slate

Accent palette:

- warm amber for truth / official light
- restrained crimson for danger / corruption
- muted brass or aged gold for civic authority

Rules:

- Crimson should be rare and meaningful
- Avoid purple-heavy grading
- Avoid oversaturated blues
- Keep contrast strong enough for text overlay

## Character Usage

- Do not build the image around detailed faces
- If people appear, prefer silhouettes, partial figures, or restrained portrait presence
- The scene should suggest a council, not reveal the full roster
- The viewer should feel like an observer entering a compromised institution

## Deliverables

- Desktop master: `2560x1440` minimum, ideally `2880x1620`
- Mobile crop: `1080x1350`
- Export: `WebP` and layered source file if possible
- Safe area for website text on top of the image

## Acceptance Checklist

The image is successful if:

- it reads clearly in under one second
- it supports overlaid website copy without becoming illegible
- it feels specific to Rotten Council, not generic noir
- it looks premium without relying on visual noise
- it creates intrigue without spoiling the game

## Short Prompt Version

"A premium civic-noir website hero image for a deduction game about corruption inside a city council. A circular chamber or council table in an official interior, deep navy and charcoal palette, restrained amber light, minimal but meaningful crimson accents, polished wood, brass, stone, archival documents, subtle asymmetry suggesting hidden corruption, no explicit villain reveal, no action scene, no clutter, strong negative space for website title and CTA."
