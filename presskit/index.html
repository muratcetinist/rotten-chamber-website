Short Description

Rotten Chamber is a single-player social deduction puzzle game. Analyze role-based statements around a council table, test your theories with targeted abilities and arrest every corrupt official before three wrong calls end the investigation.

Store Description

A single-player social deduction puzzle game where you read a corrupt city council through logic alone. Cross-check statements, use investigation abilities and arrest the corrupt before three mistakes or one bad Mayor arrest ends the case.

Medium Description

Rotten Chamber reimagines social deduction as a solo puzzle game. Each official at the table speaks according to their role. Clean officials tell the truth. Corrupt officials lie, disguise themselves, silence witnesses, blackmail neighbors or quietly turn allies against you. Your job is to cross-reference every statement, test key suspects with limited abilities and make the right arrests before the chamber collapses into chaos.

Long Description

Rotten Chamber is a single-player social deduction puzzle game set around a council table full of officials, witnesses and hidden corruption. Every seat produces information. Every claim can be checked against another. Some officials are truthful. Others are lying on purpose. The challenge is to separate solid testimony from planted deception and build a case strong enough to survive your next arrest.

The game takes the language of social deduction and strips away bluffing, table talk and group play. What remains is a logic puzzle built from roles, contradictions, adjacency rules and hidden corruption effects. Corrupt officials do not only lie. They also disguise themselves as clean roles, silence crucial voices, force innocent neighbors to speak falsely and convert trusted allies into new threats.

Players investigate each chamber by reading statements, using active role abilities and deploying one-shot professionals that can reveal alignment, expose lie status or reset a spent ability. The cost of a bad call is always present: three wrong arrests end the case and arresting an innocent Mayor ends it immediately.

Rotten Chamber includes a 45-level campaign across three acts, custom games with adjustable table size and corrupt count, deterministic seeds and shareable level codes, an in-game annotation system for tracking deductions and English/Turkish language support. It is built for players who enjoy reasoning through information, spotting contradictions and solving cases through logic rather than luck.

Boilerplate

Rotten Chamber is an in-development single-player social deduction puzzle game for PC. Built in Godot, it challenges players to investigate a city council through role-based statements, hidden corruption effects and high-stakes arrests. With a 45-level campaign, custom game options, deterministic case sharing and full English/Turkish support, Rotten Chamber is designed for players who want the structure of a logic puzzle with the tension of a social deduction game.

Key Features

  • Social deduction rebuilt as a single-player logic puzzle
  • 15 roles with distinct statement logic, including 5 corrupt roles with disruptive powers
  • 45 campaign levels across 3 acts with escalating complexity and difficulty
  • Active investigation abilities that verify truthfulness, compare alignments or count corruption within a group
  • One-shot hired professionals that help break deadlocks without replacing deduction
  • High-stakes arrest structure: three mistakes lose the case and an innocent Mayor arrest is an instant failure
  • Custom games with adjustable table size, corrupt count, difficulty and role setup
  • Deterministic seeds and shareable level codes for replayable and reproducible cases
  • Card stamps and color ribbons for player-driven note taking and deduction tracking
  • Full tutorial support and English/Turkish localization

Why It Stands Out

  • Rotten Chamber delivers the feel of social deduction without requiring other players.
  • Its tension comes from logic pressure and irreversible arrests, not reflexes or random combat outcomes.
  • Corruption changes how information behaves: lies can spread, witnesses can be silenced and trusted roles can be compromised.
  • The political chamber setting gives the game a distinct identity compared to village, party or sci-fi social deduction formats.

Press FAQ

What is Rotten Chamber?
Rotten Chamber is a single-player social deduction puzzle game in which players investigate a city council by reading role-based statements, testing claims and arresting corrupt officials.
How is it different from traditional social deduction games?
Traditional social deduction games depend on bluffing, social reads and group discussion. Rotten Chamber keeps the hidden-role tension but rebuilds it as a solo logic puzzle where outcomes are driven by rules, contradictions and deduction.
Is Rotten Chamber multiplayer?
No. Rotten Chamber is built specifically as a single-player experience.
What kind of progression does the game have?
The game includes a 45-level campaign across three acts, plus custom games that let players change table size, corruption count, difficulty and role setup.
Does the game support replayability?
Yes. Custom games, deterministic seeds and shareable level codes make it easy to replay or share exact puzzle setups.
